# External UI/UX References

These references are not for copying. They are for extracting patterns that fit Alisnart: credibility, visual proof, project depth, and conversion.

## Reference Categories

### 1. Professional Lighting Design Studios

Use these for credibility, service framing, and project-depth structure.

- Lighting Design Group - https://www.ldg.com/
  - Pattern to learn: service segmentation by business type, strong professional positioning, project-scale confidence.
  - Apply to Alisnart: split services into scenic night tourism, commercial street activation, city renewal, hotel/resort entrance, and performance/event lighting.

- Available Light - https://www.availablelight.com/
  - Pattern to learn: concise studio voice, broad project-type coverage, design professionalism without overexplaining.
  - Apply to Alisnart: keep copy direct; show that design, engineering, control, and installation are integrated.

- Oldner Lighting - https://www.oldnerlighting.com/
  - Pattern to learn: architectural lighting credibility and focused project categories.
  - Apply to Alisnart: avoid cluttered category names; make service labels obvious to procurement clients.

- Oculus Light Studio - https://oculuslightstudio.com/
  - Pattern to learn: studio trust, architectural lighting language, polished case presentation.
  - Apply to Alisnart: add case facts, location type, material/control specs, and outcome in a quiet professional layout.

### 2. Experiential / Public Art / Exhibition Studios

Use these for immersive storytelling, project photography, and visitor-flow thinking.

- Pendulee - https://pendulee.com/
  - Pattern to learn: museum exhibit and public art positioning with strong project thumbnails.
  - Apply to Alisnart: each product page should feel like a place-based installation, not only a product card.

- ETCxDesign - https://www.etcxdesign.com/
  - Pattern to learn: exhibitions explained through environment, purpose, and visitor experience.
  - Apply to Alisnart: describe night-tour routes, photo points, stay time, interaction, and operating rhythm.

### 3. High-End Atmosphere / Performance Lighting

Use these for emotional tone and visual atmosphere, but keep Alisnart more practical and engineering-oriented.

- LiteFlair - https://www.liteflair.com/
  - Pattern to learn: light as atmosphere, hospitality and experiential language.
  - Apply to Alisnart: use atmosphere in hero visuals, but always pair it with installable structures and maintenance logic.

- Design DarKo - https://www.designdarko.com/about
  - Pattern to learn: award/studio positioning and performance-lighting confidence.
  - Apply to Alisnart: use stronger claims only when backed by case evidence or measurable experience.

## What Not To Copy

- Oversized decorative hero sections that hide actual products.
- Abstract gradients instead of project images.
- Portfolio-only navigation with no procurement path.
- Minimal pages that look premium but fail to explain specs, budget, installation, or maintenance.
- Mobile menus that depend on fragile hidden state or tiny tap targets.

## Alisnart Reference Principles

1. Real scene first: the first viewport should reveal a real or realistic installation in context.
2. Engineering after emotion: every beautiful scene needs material, structure, control, and maintenance proof nearby.
3. Procurement path always visible: quote/contact/WhatsApp should be reachable without hunting.
4. Product pages must be reusable as proposal modules.
5. Case pages must be understandable by both clients and AI-search systems.
